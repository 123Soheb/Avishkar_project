Rail Fence Cipher

The Rail Fence Cipher is a type of transposition cipher that rearranges the order of letters in the plaintext to create ciphertext. Here's how it works:

  1. Key: The key is the number of "rails" or rows used in the encryption. For example, if the key is 3, there will be three rows for encrypting the message.
  2. Encryption: To encrypt a message, the plaintext is written in a zigzag pattern along the specified number of rails. Then, the letters are read off row by row to create the ciphertext.
  3. Decryption: To decrypt the ciphertext, the recipient needs to know the key (number of rails). The ciphertext is written in the same zigzag pattern along the rails, and the plaintext is read off row by row.

Example

Let's encrypt the message "HELLO" using the Rail Fence Cipher with a key of 3. First, we arrange the letters in a zigzag pattern along three rails: 

        H . . O . . 
        . E . L . .
        . . L . . .

Now, we read the ciphertext row by row:

        HOOELLL

So, "HELLO" encrypted using the Rail Fence Cipher with a key of 3 becomes "HOOELLL."

The security of this cipher depends on the key, and it's important to keep the key secret. Increasing the number of rails (making the fence taller) can enhance security.

Advantages: - Provides a basic level of security. - Easy to understand and implement.

Disadvantages: - Vulnerable to frequency analysis. - Security depends on the key (number of rails) and its secrecy.
